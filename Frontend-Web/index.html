RSI (Relative Strength Index)

A momentum oscillator that measures the speed and change of price movements.

RSI is typically used to identify overbought or oversold conditions in a market.

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

A trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of a security’s price.

MACD is used to spot changes in the strength, direction, momentum, and duration of a trend in a stock's price.

MA50 (50-Day Moving Average)

An indicator that averages out the closing prices for the last 50 days and updates with each new closing price.

It's used to analyze the mid-term trend and determine support or resistance levels.

MA9 (9-Day Moving Average)

A short-term moving average that averages the closing prices over the past 9 days.

Often used by traders to spot short-term trend reversals and as a component of the MACD.
