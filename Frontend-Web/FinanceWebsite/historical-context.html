The Origins of Modern Finance

The foundation of modern finance was laid during the late 19th and early 20th centuries, where innovations in banking and investment practices began to shape the American financial landscape.

The Great Depression and Its Aftermath

The Great Depression of the 1930s was a pivotal moment in American history, leading to significant financial reforms and the establishment of regulatory bodies such as the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC).

The Post-War Economic Boom

Following World War II, the United States experienced a period of unprecedented economic growth, during which consumer credit and mortgage financing flourished, significantly impacting American lifestyles.

The Era of Financialization

Starting in the late 20th century, the U.S. economy saw a shift towards financialization, a process where financial markets, institutions, and norms gained greater influence over economic policy and economic outcomes.

The 2008 Financial Crisis

The 2008 financial crisis, sparked by the collapse of the housing bubble, led to a global economic downturn and prompted widespread scrutiny and reform of financial practices and regulations.
